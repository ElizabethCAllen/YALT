Creating a Professional Resume

While there are a few commonly used resume styles, your resume should reflect your unique education, experience and relevant skills. You might consider having multiple versions of your resume tailored to the jobs youâ€™re applying for. Here are a few key resume writing tips that will help you organize and design your resume.

  1. Look for keywords in the job posting
      Include those keywords in your resume where relevant. If you are applying for a job at a restaurant, then including great customer service skills would be smart to include in your resume.
  2. Make it simple and Easy to Read
  3. Make it Brief
      Your employer does not have time nor do they want to read a 3 page resume.
  4. Use a Professional Font
      Times New Roman, or Arial are good font examples. Selecting a clear, readable font will help make your resume appear more professional.
  5. Include only Relevant Information
      The most important information should also be listed first.
  6. Proofread and Edit
      Some employers will automatically get rid of resumes if there are spelling errors so make sure you triple check your resume before sending.

Your resume is often the first step to getting an interview with an employer. By following these steps you are one step closer to nailing that job! and remember, there is only one you and you are amazing!
