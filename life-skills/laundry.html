How to do Laundry

List of laundry essential

  1. Fels naptha
  2. Borax powder
  3. Ammonia
  4. Detergent
  5. Baking soda
  6. Shout stain remover
  7. Spray starch
  8. Spray bottle of water
  9. Clothing iron
  10. hangers
  11. Laundry bin

How to remove excess odor from clothing

Soak clothing in a mixture of ammonia, fels naptha, borax, baking soda, and water. Leave the clothing in the chemical mix for a few days, if it is summer leave the mixture out in the sun. If the water becomes cloudy within minutes of putting the clothing in, pull the clothing out of the mix and rinse in a bucket or bowl until the water runs clear, then place the clothing back in the chemical soak. Once properly soaked, wash the clothing in the washer in the same mixture with detergent with an extra rinse to wash out any residual chemicals.

General laundry cleaning practices

About one tablespoon of fels naphtha scraped off the bar and added to a load of clothes will help clean and deodorize your items.

Wash most clothes in tap-temperature water. Bed linens and towels can be washed in hot water. Hot water wrecks elastic and synthetics.

Wash clothes with zippers or other rough areas separately from knits to avoid snagging and pilling.

If you have clothes with pilling, they do make little shavers to remove the pills and restore the look, or you can do it with a razor, like the ones you shave with, just be careful not to damage the clothing.

Wash clothes inside out to keep the color fresh and the surface looking new.

Change bed linens once a week in warm weather and every two weeks in cool weather, at the least. If you are sick, you may want to change them more often.

Bleach sets rust stains. Rust stains and dirt stains can be removed with a salt and lemon juice paste, white vinegar, vinegar and borax paste, cream of tartar and peroxide, or a rust remover from the laundry aisle of the store. Try naval jelly from the hardware store on a test spot and if it doesn’t eat the cloth, brush a little on the rust stain and wash it out after about two minutes.

There is also a rhubarb method of removing rust.

Wash dark colored clothing separate from light colored clothing. Do not mix them in the drier, either.

Dishwasher soap makes a good stain remover and is a strong cleaner in general

Use Spray and Wash or Shout on grease spots on clothing just as soon as you take the clothes off. Keep a bottle in your bath or bedroom for that purpose.

Check treated grease spots before you put clothes in the drier. If the spot remains, treat it again, let it rest, then rewash it. Putting it in the drier sets the stain.

Another way to treat grease stains is to put straight liquid laundry soap on the spot.

Add borax to loads of towels, sheets, undies, and linens to sanitize them.

Bleach is tough on fabric, so don’t use it often. It does whiten and sanitize cloth.

Your drier may have a hot sanitizing cycle. Use it for old clothes or germy loads. If lice come home with someone, this cycle can be used on pillows and stuffed animals.
