Cleaning Tips and Tricks

List of cleaning essentials

  1. Various sized scrub brushes
  2. Off brand magic erasers
  3. Scouring powder
  4. Liquid bleach
  5. Ammonia
  6. Lime away
  7. White vinegar
  8. Glass cleaner
  9. Mop
  10. Broom
  11. Toilet brush
  12. Scraping tool
  13. Sponges
  14. Paper towels
  15. Bucket for water

There are many home-made cleaners that are nontoxic. Vinegar, carbonated water, baking soda, peroxide, alcohol, ammonia, dish soap, and hot water can be used for everyday household cleaning chores.

Peroxide’s bubbling action can remove grime from some stubborn surfaces and small spaces that are hard to clean. It can be used as a drain cleaner, as well. Peroxide can bleach cloth, so take care not to accidentally spot clothing.

Ammonia is good at dissolving grease and cleaning windows and ovens. Beware its fumes, however. Don’t breathe it. Dilute it or use it outdoors.

Baking soda water is a good general cleaner and when left on tough dirt for a half hour, it can work wonders.

Vinegar can help remove lime and mineral deposits on glass or built-up soapy layers. It’s often used as a glass cleaner

Alcohol can dissolve ink and many other hard-to-remove substances.

Dish soap and hot water is good for general cleaning, except for glass. Do rinse it off or it will attract dirt.

Carbonated water is bubbly and slightly acidic, It can remove stains from coffee pots and clean hard-to-reach areas like screw threads on bottles and lids.

Traditional cleaning products

Tile, porcelain, and steel can be cleaned with abrasive scouring powders like Comet or Dutch Cleanser. These usually contain some bleach, as well. Be careful that you don’t use this on softer surfaces like glass or plastic that can be scratched by the abrasive.

Windex is one of many window cleaning products that all work pretty well on windows and can also be used on almost anything.

There are special cleaners made for many tricky surfaces, such as upholstery, carpet, floors, marble, or wood.

Painted surfaces should be cleaned with a mild soap and not rubbed too much because paint loss can result.

Lime-away is good at dissolving lime and mineral deposits in the bathroom and kitchen or on windows. It does have awful fumes and should not be used without adequate ventilation. There are also other similar products

Bleach is the best at killing germs. You just can’t beat it. It does have some fumes that you probably should not breathe. It will spot or destroy cloth. It can eat right through a pair of jeans, given a bit of time. Use it in the kitchen and bathroom, as well as on doorknobs and other areas frequently touched by people, such as the panel on a microwave or a refrigerator handle.

There are way too many products in the cleaning and laundry aisle at the grocery store. There is probably something there for almost any need.

Spic and Span powder is a vintage favorite, as are Lysol, Fantastic, and Ajax.

GENERAL CLEANING TIPS

Wash doorknobs once a week with bleach.

Remove shoes before entering your home to keep dirt out of the house.

Use a Dustbuster on upholstered furniture to remove pet hair and dirt.

Pour a cup of bleach down every drain once a week to prevent clogs and keep bugs from entering via the drain. If you are going away for awhile, pour more bleach down the drain than usual.

To prevent insects from entering your house, sprinkle borax liberally around the foundations, then wet it lightly or mist with water.

Choose tile or wood floors over carpet. Carpet is impossible to clean well.

Cover hard floor surfaces with washable rugs over nonslip liners. This keeps wood floors from having to be refinished as often.

Bleach can get rid of dark mold but can also cause spores to be released.

Clothing left wet or damp for a few days can grow a fungus called mildew which smells and leaves little black spots that are hard to get rid of.

Use bamboo skewers to reach areas difficult to clean.

Dusting just scatters the dust. Use a damp cloth to wipe areas instead.so you can get it out of the house.
