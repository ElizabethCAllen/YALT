Jumping Starting your Car

There will come a point where your vehicle will have a dead battery. You should have a pair of jumper cables for your car at all times. You must always use the jumper cables with caution because you can get an electric shock.

First, you will find a helpful that has a working battery. This will be the only way you can actually jump your car without having to get another battery.

Two cars attempting to jump the other car.

Next, you will need to make sure the positions of the vehicle are correct so that the jumper cables reach each battery terminal. Some battery terminals have covers on them, so make sure to take them off before applying the jumper cables.

Make sure your vehicle is off because there shouldn't be any electricity going into the batteries. Open the hood of each vehicle so that the batteries are exposed. The red (positive) cable should go onto the positive post of the good battery. The other red cable connects to the dead battery. Then take the black (negative) cable and connect it to the negative post. Finally, take the other black clamp and put it on a grounded surface of the vehicle with the dead battery.

Jumper cables connected to the vehicles

The vehicle with the good battery should now be turned on and should sit idling for a couple minutes. The vehicle with the dead battery can be started. After a couple minutes, the jumper cables can come off. There is a specific way for the cables to come off of it:

  1. Take the black clamp off of the grounded surface,
  2. Take the other black clamp from the negative post from the good battery,
  3. Take the red clamp off of the 'dead' battery terminal
  4. Take the other red clamp from the good battery.
